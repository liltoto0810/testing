Section 1

Where do visitors to Malaysia come from?

Start with source markets. The first four visuals separate market size, regional concentration, recovery from 2019 and short-term momentum from 2024 to 2025.

Market size alone can hide vulnerability: a strong nearby source base gives volume, but concentration increases exposure if a few markets slow down.

Section 2

Which states are best equipped to support tourism?

Capacity is not just the number of hotels. The dashboard compares physical supply, guest composition, occupancy tightness and seasonal volatility.

Section 3

Which states show the strongest tourism demand?

Demand is normalised by room supply to reveal pressure. This avoids treating large-capacity states as automatically stronger performers.

The pressure index is derived as total hotel guests divided by hotel rooms. It is a demand-intensity measure, not a direct occupancy measure.

Section 4

How vulnerable is Malaysia’s tourism economy to major disruptions?

The final section links market concentration and state pressure to the receipts shock. It reframes tourism as a system where growth and risk move together.

The deepest disruption reduced receipts by roughly 99.7 index points from the 2019 baseline; recovery by 2024 does not remove future shock exposure.
